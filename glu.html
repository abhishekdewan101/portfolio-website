GLU Enabling cross cultural exchanges amongst students.

Project Description

GLU is a social platform that allows its user to have controversial conversations in an attempt to clear out that exsist between people from different cultural backgrounds. Inspired by personal experiences, the GLU app provides the user with a safe place to hold conversations with other users of a different cultural group with the end goal being increased cross cultural exchanges. The idea is that once users clear out their doubts about the other culture, they might be more open to having normal converstaions with people from other cultural backgrounds as themselves.

Design Process

User Interviews

The first phase of the project consisted of researching prior work that had been done in the field of increasing cross cultural exchanges between students of different cultural background. Using the research as a starting point, the team conducted 10 interviews with students from Indian and Chineese cultural backgrounds. These cultural backgrounds were chosen as they represented the two largest cultural groups of our cohort.

Brainstorming

Using the insights from the research and user interviews, the team decided to perform a thought wall exercise. A thought wall is a small scale version of a affinity wall and helped the team narrow down a particular problem faced by the group of students. It was discovered that students from different cultural backgrounds tend to converse less with each other due to reasons like shyness, no knowledge of how to approach them, etc. In order to further understand what our users go through we created an empathy map that allowed the team to map what the user hears and feels to what he does and say. This allowed to create a more comprehensive solution while designing.

Wireframes

Next steps involved the creation of whiteboard based wireframes. We dediced to use a whiteboard for our wireframes as it allowed us the flexibility to be able to change things around easily. Moreover the team felt that the whiteboard allowed for a more cohesive discussion on the pros and cons of each design decision being made by the team.

Final Prototypes

The final deliverable for this project came in the form of medium fidelity prototypes created using Balsamiq. The prototypes were printed and used to perform some user testing in order to get feedback on the good and bad things about the design. The team however, did not have enough time to incorporate the feedback.
